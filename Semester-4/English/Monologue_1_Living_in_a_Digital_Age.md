# Monologue on Living in a Digital Age

## Step 1. Introduction

There is a saying: "Technology is not just a tool — it is a mirror of how we live." I think this is very true today. We use digital technologies every day, and they change how we communicate, study, and work. ***Firstly***, I want to talk about what the Digital Age is. ***Moreover***, I will speak about smart machines. ***Finally***, I will discuss smart cities and homes.

## Step 2. The Digital Age

2.1. The Digital Age, ***also known as*** the Information Age, is a time when **information media** became very important for the whole world. The main difference from previous times is that now all data can **be quantitatively encoded as a series of ones and zeroes** and sent anywhere in the world. **The advent of personal computers** in the 1970s and 1980s started this big change. It gave millions of people the ability to work with information at home and in the office.

2.2. ***In addition***, there were several important events in the history of the Digital Age. **The development of fiber optic cables** and microprocessors helped **accelerate the transmission and processing of information**. The Internet became an **interactive consumer exchange for goods and information**, and email made possible **near-instant exchange of information** across the world. ***As a result***, the **digitization of information** has **had a profound impact on** many industries like music and publishing. Today almost everything can **be described in digital form**.

## Step 3. Smart machines

3.1. Smart machines are devices with **machine-to-machine (M2M) technologies** and **cognitive computing technologies** **embedded in** them, ***such as*** artificial intelligence and machine learning. They can **work through tasks without human intervention**, which is very useful. ***On the one hand***, **robotic automation in manufacturing facilities** makes production faster and cheaper. ***On the other hand***, these technologies can **dramatically change the nature of work and other societal norms**. ***Because of*** their ability **to automate some human tasks**, many people worry that it may **lead to a high rate of unemployment**.

3.2. ***For example***, IBM Watson is a **cognitive computing system** that can **collect and analyze an unprecedented volume of data**. Doctors use it **to diagnose diseases and recommend the best treatments**. So smart machines can help people, not just replace them. ***Nevertheless***, there are still many questions about how to use them correctly and safely.

## Step 4. Smart cities and homes

4.1. A smart city is a city that uses technology to make life better for people. ***Furthermore***, **smart city infrastructure** includes **cloud infrastructure**, **Wi-Fi deployment**, **mobile edge computing (MEC)**, **5G network architecture**, and **small cell deployment**. Some cities even create a **programmable citywide testbed** to test new technologies. I live in Saint Petersburg, and I think it is slowly becoming smarter, ***although*** many things still need to be improved.

4.2. Smart homes use **wired and wireless systems** to **facilitate communication between the user and the system**. People can buy different **gizmos and gadgets** with **internet connectivity** and voice control. I would like to live in a smart home where I can control lights, temperature, and security from my phone. I think it would make everyday life much easier and more comfortable.

## Step 5. Creative Thinking

***Apart from*** what I already discussed, I think Web3 and blockchain are very interesting new technologies. ***Not only*** can they make data more secure, ***but also*** they can change how we do financial transactions. I am interested in this topic because I study information security, and I believe blockchain will play an important role in the future of cybersecurity and data protection.

## Step 6. Conclusion

***To sum up***, the Digital Age changed how people share and use information. Smart machines help in many areas but also bring new problems. ***At the same time***, smart cities and homes make our life more comfortable. ***In short***, technology will keep changing our world, and we need to be ready for it.
